
# History

The idea of Boosting dates back to the 1980s, as a way to combine multiple weak models (shallow Decision Trees)
in order to build a more powerful one, leveraging the accumulated errors generated in the intermediate process.
The first algorithm that achieved this outcome was the AdaBoost, developed in collaboration between Yoav Freund and Robert Schapire. For this breakthrough, they were awarded the Gödel Prize in 2003.

Skipping to 1999, Jerome Friedman took a different perspective on Boosting. He viewed Boosting
as a "gradient descent optimization problem", that is, one must minimize the loss by tweaking functions
which will point to the negative gradient (the direction the error decreases). Friedman was also able to generalize the problem for different tasks, such as Regression, Classification and Ranking.
